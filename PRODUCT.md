# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Administrators, Officers, Head Admins, Group Leaders (GL), and On-Ground Admins within
the Burhani Galli Kothari (BGK) ladies' community organization. Users are community
insiders who manage a known membership list and track attendance at religious events
(Ashara Mubaraka programs, Muharram gatherings, milad programs). Used on personal
Android phones, primarily at the venue during or right after an event.

## Product Purpose

Internal operations and attendance management app for a Dawoodi Bohra community ladies'
organization. Core jobs: mark attendance at events, view member details and history,
generate attendance reports, manage user accounts and member records. Success means
administrators always have accurate attendance data on their phone without spreadsheets
or paper registers.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, Organized, Warm. The app serves a close-knit religious community — it
should feel as reliable as a well-kept register, as warm as a familiar face, and as
organized as the community it serves. Not flashy. Not cold. Purple is the existing brand
color — a dignified, community-appropriate choice.

## Anti-references

- Consumer social apps (Instagram-style gradients, story-circles, neon accents)
- Generic SaaS dashboards (cold blues, hero-metric number cards, chart-for-chart's-sake)
- Overly minimal blank-canvas designs (real data needs real hierarchy)
- Dark mode UIs (used in ambient daylight at community venues, not a control room)
- Enterprise admin panels (this is a community tool, not a CRM)

## Design Principles

1. **Data, not decoration**: every visual element earns its place by helping users
   understand member or event data faster. No decorative chrome.
2. **Role-appropriate clarity**: what you can act on should be obvious; what you cannot
   should be invisible, not just disabled.
3. **Community over corporate**: warmth over polish. The app serves real relationships.
   Status colors (green/amber/red) carry meaning; they never appear decoratively.
4. **Trust through consistency**: same color always means the same thing, everywhere.
   Violations erode user confidence in the data.
5. **Mobile-first density**: users scan lists on phones while standing at events. 
   Information density and thumb-reach matter more than generous whitespace.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Status information always conveyed through both color AND icon/label
(never color alone). Right-to-left considerations not required. Reduced motion: no
gratuitous animation — transitions exist only when they carry navigational meaning.
